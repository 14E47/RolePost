People Management is one thing that intrigues a lot of us, but Micky Chopra is the right person if you really want to understand the nuances of this skill. He has built a career in Human Resource management but this was not his natural instinct when he was in his college.

Micky started his professional career with Indian Navy Services, after completing his MCA from Galgotia College (Lucknow University). The technical knowledge he received in the college came in handy for understanding client requirements and this helped him move to FCS Software. While working as the resource manager at FCS, he was able to understand how it is important to be able to manage people to improve processes.

The journey towards Human Resource was a slow and steady transformation, where he with his goal oriented focus was able to carve his way to becoming a talent specialist. His first break in Talent management was with SAVY Infotech as the Country Manager in Talent Acquisition and Client Delivery.  Talent Acquisition was an exciting domain for him but his desire to dwell deeper into the realms of people management made him see beyond talent acquisition.

For last three years, he working with OSS cube and then Taction Software LLC has taken a strategic role, where he understand that talent acquisition is just the start of the cycle and you need to look deeper to sustain the bottom line. He acknowledges how retention and talent engagement can lead to a sustained growth of an organisation.  He approaches human resources in a very technical way and attributes his success to his technical understanding, which has helped him build his career.

The way HR has changed with time is phenomenal,   from being a backend role it has today become a strategy based position with multiple elements like talent branding, talent satisfaction, talent performance and business acquisition through talent being the key matrices today. As Taction Software deals in Niche Technologies, the task becomes more challenging and Talent Branding plays a very important role in acquiring the right talent.

The biggest motivators for Micky have been his family who supported this tough decision of moving from the more lucrative technology domain to people management.  Today, he is amongst the top 50 HR Technical leaders in the country and a very active speaker about technology in HR. He also promotes sports in his work sphere and at Oss cube was the president of the sports initiative and helped build a culture for sports. This he adds also helps build better work ethics.

“The intent of Rolepost is great, and will help bring the community together and this will be also a great way to appreciate the real talent”

Our teenage days are full of dreams and in the race of life, we tend to forget them and choose a conventional career path. There are only a few who start living their dreams since their childhood. Priyanka Aggarwal had always imagined herself flying to exotic locations and started working her way towards her dream in her school days.

With her father in the merchant navy, she got fascinated to international travel and understanding different cultures. She started working as a promoter in her school days and after her first year of college at Delhi College of Arts and Commerce; she joined an air hostess training school. Even before her graduation, she was offered a chance to fly with a leading airline. After flying with domestic carriers, she was within 2 years promoted to as an instructor. This was a great learning for Priyanka Aggarwal and also opened a new door of opportunity.

After getting an offer from Charter Airlines, she joined the services and was able to fulfil her childhood dream of flying across the world. In this journey, she had an opportunity to work for dignitaries of highest order, actors, and politicians. The life was not easy as she was travelling for days in continuity, from one continent to another but she enjoyed every bit of her work.

The best thing which she maintained all her life was to stay grounded and learn from every experience.

In 2011, after a 7-year long career as a flight attendant she wanted to explore something new and was tired of the harsh schedule in flying. This meant to explore a completely new territory. She joined the hospitality industry with ‘Samhi Group’ and very well adapted to her new role as an Admin Coordinator. She found a great similarity in two industries and soon was offered to join in as Executive Assistant to the MD at Great India Nautanki Company. With the biggest entertainment destination “Kingdom Of Dreams”, Multiple Restaurants and Hotels under their stable, she got exposure to management of these properties. She handled multiple projects like bringing the First Reverse Bungee to India, opening a speciality restaurant in Bangalore and managing the PNL for Kingdom Of Dreams and Galaxy.

With her interest in Sales and Marketing, she joined Zenica Group as an executive assistant to the Sales and Marketing Head. Zenica Group is amongst the biggest dealerships for Porsche and Audi in India and also owns multiple Apple Reseller Stores. Priyanka understood the nuances of the business and how to manage a team to get sustained growth. This experience transformed her as a professional and made her realise the difference she can make to any business.

With a focus to transform the business practices at her previous organisation, she joined “Kingdom Of Dreams” again but this time, she was leading alliances. She in this last stint has been able to build the complete hotel division at “Kingdom Of Dreams” and is now working to build synergies with Event Management Companies to make KOD, the number one destination for mega events.

Her perseverance and hunger to learn are infectious and we at RolePost wish the best for her future.

But everything wasn’t fancy for him from the start. Being a commerce student, it wasn’t easy for him to take a decision to enter into a line totally different from his current degree. Following the repercussions of this economic hit, his clients across the globe had to close down which then led to him shutting down the operations of his own company.

At this point, looking at the depressed macroeconomic conditions, Nishant did not think it was viable for him to come up with another venture of his and so instead he decided to venture into untested waters and hence joined the then fledgling startup Gozoop. “Self Evaluation is a key to make challenging time, pave the way to what you really want.”

Varun Ranjan, a graduate from Delhi College of Engineering, was an exceptional student all his life and soon after graduation joined an IT firm working in financial domain. Always focussed on his career he worked with the company for nearly 2 years, but due to the economic slowdown, the work also slowed down and Varun had to be on ‘bench’ for some time.  Just at the beginning of his career, he was full of enthusiasm and wanted to make a dent. But, this dampened his ambitions and also gave him time to re-evaluate what he really wants to do.

Being an athlete at the national level in his schooling days, he always wanted to work in sports. Sports did not look like a viable career option and thus like all his peers he also did his engineering, to save guard his career. This discontent from his job made him venture into the field he really wanted and he against all odds he did his masters from Master of Advanced Studies in Sports Administration & Technology from Geneva.

As he rightly says

“You are always getting prepared to can take that Plunge”.

He was able to convince family and friends and embarked on a journey of sports management.

 

He has worked for 5 years in different domains of sports management across Europe. Being a part of the International Olympics Committee was like a childhood dream come true for him. Working as a Project officer at International Olympics Committee, he got exposure to multiple sports and understood the market potential of every sport. With a desire to build a property from the ground up, he joined AIBA (International Boxing Association). Even though he has not boxed ever in his life, he found this challenge extremely interesting and he helped develop boxing in multiple countries.

On asking him about the potential of sports Industry in India, he exclaims:

“Absolutely it is. Sports industry in India has grown exponentially over the last 5-6 years and it has no signs of stopping. There are more and more jobs coming in this space and what is also heartening to see is that people are starting their own ventures in the sports space”.

He feels this is the right time to invest in Indian Sports Industry and is keen to work in India in near future.

Varun is looking forward to FIFA U-17 World Cup coming to India and thinks of it as the biggest sporting events to happen in India. He comments

“There is a big event happening in India in 2017 but unfortunately many people don’t know about it.”

And he hopes to be a part of it. Wishing the best to Varun and hoping Indian Sports Industry can boom and many people can get involved in their dream jobs in India.
